1. Domain
The domain is the part of an email address that comes after the "@" symbol. For example, in the email address "example@gmail.com", "gmail.com" is the domain.
In the context of email, a domain is essentially the identity of the organization or entity that owns the email address.



2. HasMX
MX stands for Mail Exchanger. It's a type of DNS (Domain Name System) record that directs email messages to the correct email server.
"HasMX" indicates whether a domain has a valid MX record configured. This is essential for receiving email messages.

3. HasDMARC
DMARC stands for Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting, and Conformance. It's a security protocol that helps prevent email spoofing and phishing attacks.
"HasDMARC" indicates whether a domain has a valid DMARC record configured. This record specifies how the domain owner wants email providers to handle unauthenticated emails that claim to come from their domain.

4. HasSPF
SPF stands for Sender Policy Framework. It's a security protocol that helps prevent email spoofing by specifying which email servers are authorized to send emails on behalf of a domain.
"HasSPF" indicates whether a domain has a valid SPF record configured. This record lists the IP addresses or email servers that are authorized to send emails from the domain.
